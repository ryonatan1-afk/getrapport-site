About this holiday

What is Rosh Hashanah?

Rosh Hashanah, the Jewish New Year, marks the beginning of the High Holy Days and the start of the Jewish year. Observed on the first and second days of Tishrei, it is a time of synagogue services, the sounding of the shofar (ram's horn), and reflection on the year gone by and the one ahead. Traditions include dipping apples in honey to symbolise hopes for a sweet new year, eating round challah, and greeting loved ones with 'Shanah Tovah' — wishing them a good year. It is a deeply meaningful holiday of spiritual renewal and community.
