About this holiday

What is Shavuot?

Shavuot, meaning 'weeks,' is the Jewish holiday that falls fifty days after Passover, commemorating the giving of the Torah to Moses at Mount Sinai — one of the foundational moments in Jewish history. Observed for one or two days, Shavuot is marked by all-night Torah study sessions (Tikkun Leil Shavuot), synagogue services, and the traditional eating of dairy foods such as cheesecake and blintzes. It also has agricultural roots as a harvest festival, celebrating the first fruits of the season in the Land of Israel.
