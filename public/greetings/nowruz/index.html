About this holiday

What is Nowruz?

Nowruz, meaning 'new day' in Persian, is the Persian New Year and one of humanity's oldest celebrations, with roots stretching back over 3,000 years. Celebrated on the spring equinox (around March 20–21), it is observed by over 300 million people in Iran, Afghanistan, Kurdistan, Azerbaijan, Central Asia, and diaspora communities worldwide — across different religions and cultures. The celebration centres around the Haft-Seen table, a spring cleaning of the home, family gatherings, visits, and the lighting of bonfires. Nowruz represents renewal, nature's rebirth, and the triumph of light.
