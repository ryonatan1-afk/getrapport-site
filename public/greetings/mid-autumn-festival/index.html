About this holiday

What is Mid-Autumn Festival?

The Mid-Autumn Festival, also known as the Moon Festival or Mooncake Festival, is one of the most beloved holidays in Chinese, Vietnamese, and East Asian cultures, celebrated on the 15th day of the eighth lunar month when the moon is at its brightest and fullest. It is a time of family reunion, lantern displays, moon gazing, and the sharing of mooncakes — round pastries filled with lotus paste, red bean, or salted egg yolk, symbolising unity and wholeness. The festival is steeped in myths, most famously the legend of Chang'e, the Moon Goddess.
