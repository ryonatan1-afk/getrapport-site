About this holiday

What is Onam?

Onam is the most important festival of Kerala, celebrated as a harvest festival and the homecoming of the mythical king Mahabali, whose benevolent reign is said to be the golden age of Kerala. Spanning ten days, it is marked by the creation of intricate floral carpets (pookalams), the elaborate Onam Sadhya feast of 26+ dishes served on a banana leaf, snake boat races, and folk dances. Onam is a secular festival embraced by people of all religions across Kerala and the global Malayali diaspora.
