About this holiday

What is Diwali?

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is one of the most widely celebrated holidays in the world, observed by Hindus, Jains, Sikhs, and some Buddhists across South Asia and the global diaspora. Spanning five days, it symbolises the victory of light over darkness and knowledge over ignorance, and is marked by the lighting of diyas (oil lamps), the exchange of sweets and gifts, fireworks, and family gatherings. With over a billion people celebrating, Diwali is a holiday of warmth, generosity, and new beginnings.
