# Rapport — Product Vision & Roadmap

## Vision

> Rapport is the relationship warmth layer for GTM teams — detecting the right human moment to reach out, generating the right message, and making it effortless to act — inside whatever CRM you already use.

The best GTM professionals maintain dozens of warm, human relationships simultaneously. They remember promotions, notice cultural moments, and congratulate customers on wins. Rapport makes that scalable — without making it feel automated.

---

## Market Positioning

Most GTM tools ask: *"Who should I reach out to for new business?"*
Rapport asks: *"How do I stay genuinely connected to the people I already know?"*

This is a deliberate and defensible gap. The existing landscape breaks into two camps neither of which solves this:

- **Outbound/prospecting tools** (Clay, Apollo, ZoomInfo, lemlist) — focused on finding and converting new contacts at volume. Excellent at cold outreach. Not designed for relationship warmth.
- **Enterprise relationship intelligence** (Affinity, Introhive) — focused on network mapping and data capture, built for VC/PE and large enterprises. Expensive, complex, and not GTM-sales-first.

Rapport is complementary to both camps, not competitive. It sits *after* prospecting has done its job — once a contact is in your CRM, Rapport keeps the relationship alive. It should be thought of as the tool that turns a CRM contact list into an active relationship network.

**Watch risk:** Clay is moving toward signal-based outreach workflows and could add cultural/personal moment signals over time. Rapport's durable advantage is its focus on relationship warmth for existing contacts — not volume outbound — and its cultural intelligence (holiday, personal moment detection) which is outside Clay's core DNA.

## Core Architecture (Platform-Agnostic)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│        Signal Engine            │
│  Detects moments worth acting   │
│  on across all signal types     │
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│        Content Engine           │
│  Generates contextual, personal │
│  AI-powered outreach            │
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│        CRM Adapter Layer        │
│  HubSpot → Salesforce →        │
│  Standalone (future)            │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

Business logic lives in the Signal and Content engines. CRM adapters are I/O only — contacts in, actions out.

---

## Primary Users

**Sales-first:** AEs and AMs — managing existing accounts, expansion, re-engagement, and renewal
**CS second:** CSMs — focused on retention, health, and deepening relationships

> Note: SDRs are explicitly out of scope for MVP. SDR work (prospecting, cold outreach) is Clay/Apollo territory. Rapport's value is for people managing *existing* relationships, not generating new ones.

## Signal Roadmap

Signals are the moments Rapport detects and surfaces. Prioritised by user value and build complexity.

| # | Signal | GTM Value | Data Source | Complexity |
|---|--------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Cultural / national holiday | Warm, human touchpoint | Nager.Date, Open Holidays API | ✅ MVP |
| 2 | Relationship gone cold | Re-engage dormant accounts | CRM last activity | Low |
| 3 | Deal / relationship anniversary | Expansion, renewal conversation | CRM deal data | Low |
| 4 | Renewal window approaching | Proactive retention | CRM deal close date | Low |
| 5 | Contact job change | Re-engage, expand, or save at-risk | LinkedIn / news enrichment | Medium |
| 6 | Company funding or product news | Timely congratulations, opens doors | News API / RSS | Medium |
| 7 | Mutual connection or shared interest | Warm intro context | Enrichment APIs | High |

## Content Engine

All outreach generated by Claude. Prompts are:
- Signal-aware (the tone for a holiday differs from a job change)
- Contact-aware (name, role, company, relationship history)
- User-aware (sender name, relationship context, preferred tone)
- Channel-aware (email vs. LinkedIn vs. in-CRM note)

The Content Engine is CRM-agnostic. It takes a structured `OutreachRequest` and returns a `GeneratedMessage`.

## CRM Adapter Layer

Each adapter implements a standard interface:

```
getContacts()           → Contact[]
getDeals()              → Deal[]
logActivity(contact, note)
sendDigest(user, signals)
renderInlineCard(contact)  // CRM-native UI extension
```

**Phase 1:** HubSpot (OAuth, contact sync, weekly digest email, CRM card)
**Phase 2:** Salesforce adapter (same interface, different implementation)
**Phase 3:** Standalone web app (Rapport as the system of record, syncs to CRMs)

## Key Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Reason |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Primary user | Sales (AE/AM/SDR) | Largest GTM segment, clearest ROI |
| CRM strategy | Adapter pattern from day 1 | HubSpot is the first integration, not the product |
| Holiday data | Free APIs only (MVP) | Cost control; paid enrichment later |
| AI greetings | Claude | Quality, context-awareness, Anthropic alignment |
| Activity threshold | 12 months | Signal quality over volume |
| Delivery | Weekly digest + inline CRM card | Fits existing GTM workflow |
| Competitive positioning | Complementary to Clay/ZoomInfo, not competitive | Rapport is post-prospecting; existing contacts only |
| Watch risk | Clay signal expansion | Monitor; respond by deepening cultural intelligence moat |

## North Star Metric

**Outreach sent via Rapport that receives a positive reply.**

Not opens. Not messages generated. Actual human connection made.
